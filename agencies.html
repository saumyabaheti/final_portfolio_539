Space Agencies Around The World


National Space Organizations

1. NASA: The United States

NASA's contributions to the space industry has spanned 6 decades, and has arguably been the most impactful. The organisation has successfully launched more than 200 manned flights, and they are currently working on more than 70 missions. most space exploration efforts (in the US) have been led by NASA, including the Skylab space station, a partially reusable low Earth orbital spacecraft named Space Shuttle, and the most popular human spaceflight program, Apollo Moon landing missions.


NASA has also build and launched multiple rovers on the surface of Mars, each of which has been at the forefront of groundbreaking research.


2. ESA: The EU

The European Space Agency has contributed heavily to the advancement of space research. The satellites and equipment developed by ESA have visited different planets in our solar system, including Mars Express, Cassini (developed in collaboration with NASA), which has been orbiting Saturn and its moons since 2004. ESA’s space probe named Rosetta took close pictures of comet 67P/Churyumov–Gerasimenko and sent a lander to the surface.


3. China National Space Administration: China

China's long standing space program has made several contributions to human history. For instance, China was the third country to secure human spaceflight, after the Soviets and the United States. This program was named Shenzhou, the name of the spacecraft, the fifth mission of which took the first Chinese astronaut to space.


The Chinese Space Administration has also led the launch of several important satellites and orbitors, and are continually working on further developing their space program. Often, however, the country's unstable political climate and relations affects its space industry.


4. Indian Space and Research Organization (ISRO): India

The Indian Space and Research Organization has been a dominant force in global space exploration, after having completed a whopping 111 missions. India's Mars Mission is the highlight of its space journey. It was the first nation in the world to reach the orbit of Mars on its first attempt, and the first Asian nation to do so overall.


ISRO also launched two missions to the moon, one of them completely and the other partly successful. There are several strategic missions planned for the future, in collaboration with other countries.


5. Roscomsos: Russia

Russia has been a pioneer in space travel. Russian intervention paved the way for space exploration as it is today. Roscosmos has several "first" achievements - the first satellite, the first person in space, even the first space station. It currently is a major contributor and stakeholder to the International Space Station, with other dominant forces in the space industry. The recent political stress and tension has affected their space efforts, and the future of the ISS hangs in contention.


Private Space Organizations

SpaceX

Founded by Elon Musk, SpaceX aims to make space travel accessible to all. SpaceX has led several space missions in collaboration with NASA.

Blue Origin

Blue Origin is a space company founded by Jeff Bezos, the CEO of Amazon. It focuses on developing technologies for various orbital realms.

Virgin Galactic

Founded by Richard Branson, Virgin Galactic is the world's first commercial spaceline.
